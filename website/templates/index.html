ABOUT THE PROJECT

  • Misinformation is defined as false or inaccurate information that is shared with or without attempting to mislead or be biassed. False information spreading has grown to be a significant social issue that transcends national boundaries and has an impact on many aspects of public life in an era where information moves rapidly through interconnected digital networks. Misinformation has quietly made its way onto social media and had a significant impact on many aspects, such as public health, social stability, and political debate.

  • Our project seeks to make a substantial contribution to the ongoing fight against disinformation by creating this advanced dashboard, it will enable users to identify, follow, and comprehend the dynamics of misinformation on social media.

Social media sites like Reddit and Twitter, where information is shared at an unprecedented speed, has coincided with an increase in the amount and impact of misinformation due to their availability and easy access. The volume and pace of information flow render conventional manual monitoring and analysis approaches impracticable. Therefore, the foundation of our effort is the understanding that human intervention alone is not sufficient and that human expertise and some sort of advanced technical support must coexist in order to stand against this issue.

What is Misinformation?

 

Misinformation refers to false or inaccurate information that is spread unintentionally. It can be misleading or deceptive but is not necessarily intended to cause harm. Misinformation can arise from various sources, including misunderstanding, misinterpretation, or errors in communication.

A potential new topic for a misinformation dashboard could be "COVID-19 Vaccine Myths and Misinformation." This topic would involve tracking and debunking false claims and misconceptions surrounding COVID-19 vaccines, addressing concerns about safety, efficacy, and distribution, and providing accurate information to combat misinformation and promote vaccination efforts.

 

 

Verify Your Source

 

Credible sources are reliable outlets known for providing accurate, well-researched, and trustworthy information. Authored by experts or reputable organizations, they prioritize objectivity, factual accuracy, and lack of bias. Examples include peer-reviewed academic journals, respected news organizations, and government publications. Accessing information from such sources is crucial for making informed decisions.
